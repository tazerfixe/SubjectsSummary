Minamata

Minamata is the japanese city afflicted by this tragic disease from 1932 to 1968.

Symptoms

This disease causes lack of muscle coordination, muscle weakness, numbness, loss of peripheral vision, hearing and speaking difficulties and, in severe cases insanity, paralysis, coma or death.

Origin

This disease was caused by the release of methylmercury in the industrial wastewater from a chemical factory owned by the Chisso Corporation.

The seaweeds in the river floor absorved the mercury and, when the fish ate them, they got sick. When the Minamata population ate the fish, which flavour wasn't changed, they, unknowingly, got poisoned by the mercury inside them.

Discovery

The disease was only discovered in 1956 when a little girl started to show symptoms.

Soon after, more people started to get sick as well, causing an epidemic.

Research on the disease was started and after some time they traced it back to the Chisso Corporation.

Public Reaction

When the local population found out about it, they demanded the company for compensation and to start treating the wastewater before dumping it into the river.

Compensation

At first, the compensation paid to the families of the infected and deceased ones was in the value of $1000 a year in case of infection and $3000 in case of death by the disease.

Consequences

It resulted in the poisoning and death of both humans and animals, which continued for 36 years.

In total, this incident resulted in 2265 infected people and more than 900 deceased ones, not counting with the unrecognized cases.
